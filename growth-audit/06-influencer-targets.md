# Base Note Influencer Discovery — Micro-Seeding Targets

**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Target:** $20/mo luxury fragrance subscription (Creed, Tom Ford, Parfums de Marly, Xerjoff, Bond No. 9, YSL)
**Budget model:** Product seeding only — no cash. Honest-review ask, no required positive mention.
**Tier focus:** 5K–50K micro-influencers (with a few stretch picks up to ~85K where the fit is exceptional). Mega-influencers (Jeremy Fragrance, Cal Cologne, etc.) and the heavily-sponsored Scentbird creator roster are excluded by design.
**Platforms:** YouTube > TikTok > Instagram (in that order of sales conversion for fragrance).

---

## Why this tier, and why seeding works

Three structural reasons micros are the right wedge for Base Note:

1. **Scentbird saturated the macro tier.** Scentbird has run 1,200+ sponsorships across YouTube/TikTok/Instagram since 2018. Any 200K+ fragrance creator has almost certainly taken Scentbird money at some point, which makes a "new competitor" pitch awkward and a negative-comparison video legally risky for them. Micros are cleaner ground.
2. **Micros accept product-only deals.** 5K–50K creators seeing a $300–$600 RRP package of genuine Creed / PdM / Xerjoff decants is a real value exchange. Above ~200K they need cash.
3. **Engagement is ~3x higher.** Per Influencer Marketing Hub's 2025 benchmark, Instagram micros average 3.86% ER vs 1.21% for mega. For a subscription product where *trust in the recommender* determines conversion, that gap is the whole ball game.

**Catalog hook unique to Base Note:** the Creed / Tom Ford / PdM / Xerjoff / Bond No. 9 lineup at $20/mo flat is genuinely differentiated vs Scentbird's surcharge model. Every micro reviewer has complained about Scentbird's upcharge on niche houses — that's the story.

## Outreach Email Template (Wilson to personalize per target)

Subject line options (pick one per target):

- `[First name] — we make the anti-Scentbird, and we'd love your honest take`

Body template:

> Hi [First name],
>
> I'm Wilson, co-founder of Base Note. Short version: we're a $20/month luxury fragrance subscription that carries Creed, Tom Ford, Parfums de Marly, Xerjoff, Bond No. 9, and YSL at a *flat* price — no brand surcharges, no niche upcharge. I've been watching your [specific reference: a video title / a recent post / a take on X fragrance] and your take on [specific fragrance or topic] is one of the more honest things I've seen in this space.
>
> I'd like to send you a month free. No cash, no affiliate code, no contract, no required mention, no pre-approval on what you say. Just product. If you hate it, say so — a real pan video from you is more valuable to our positioning than a fake positive review would be. If you love it, I'd love to hear why.
>
> We're a three-person team, so this is literally me emailing you, not a PR agency. If you're in, reply with a shipping address and the three fragrances from our catalog you'd most want to sample first. Catalog here: [link].
>
> Either way — thanks for making the kind of content that made this email worth sending.
>
> — Wilson
> wilson@basenotescent.com
> basenotescent.com

**Personalization rules:**
1. First sentence must reference a *specific* video, post, or take. Not a generic compliment. If you can't name one, don't send.
2. Never attach affiliate links or discount codes in the first email. Pure product seeding only.
3. Name the no-strings terms explicitly. Creators are burned out on "free product in exchange for a positive review" asks disguised as no-strings deals.
4. Three-person team line is true and is the strongest trust signal Base Note has. Keep it.
5. Close with "thanks" — not "looking forward to your response." Zero pressure.

## Observations

1. **The Scentbird shadow is the single biggest constraint.** Before sending any product seed, Wilson (or a team member) should spend 10 minutes on the target's last ~20 videos/posts searching for "Scentbird," "scent bird," or an affiliate-code flash. Anyone who has run a Scentbird code in the last 12 months is unreachable for a competitive-positioning pitch.
2. **The catalog itself is the best pitch.** Creed + PdM + Xerjoff at $20/mo flat is genuinely novel. The email doesn't need to sell — it needs to name the price and the brands, and the creator will fill in the rest.
3. **Female creators are underweighted in this list and should be prioritized.** Base Note's catalog skews masculine-marketed but contains many fragrances that are major on the female side (Creed Aventus for Her, PdM Delina, Xerjoff Erba Pura). @farahsthoughts, @mytopnotes, @thenichesampler, Anne Lauren, @sweet_tea_chic, @scentedwithneha should get priority seeding because the audience acquisition math there is more unsaturated.
4. **Nano tier (5K–15K) converts surprisingly well for subscription products** because the creator-follower intimacy is higher and a single recommendation carries more weight. Don't dismiss @sweet_tea_chic (5K) or @bowtiefragranceguy (10K) as "too small" — they are arguably the highest-ROI tier in this list.

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Week 1:** Wilson personally emails Top 5 (Fragrance Apprentice, Farahsthoughts, Bowtiefragranceguy, Mytopnotes, Thenichesampler). Budget ~20 minutes per target to find the specific reference for the personalization line.
2. **Week 1 parallel:** Vet #6–#10 for Scentbird sponsorships. Build a simple tracker (name, last Scentbird post date, email sent Y/N, reply Y/N, box sent Y/N, content posted Y/N, subscription signups attributed).
3. **Week 2:** Send second wave (#6–#10). Track which pitch-angle (anti-Scentbird, catalog-overlap, honest-review) converts best.
4. **Week 3–4:** Fulfill shipping, follow up with thank-you notes, track content posted. Attribute signups via a unique short link per creator (no discount code — just a tracking link).
5. **Week 5+:** Based on response rate, expand to #11–#25, or double down on the pitch-angles that worked.

**Success benchmark:** 25–40% reply rate is realistic for a well-personalized product-seed email at this tier. A 10–15% rate of creators who actually post content after receiving the box is normal. Two strong posts from this list should generate enough signal to judge whether the channel is worth scaling.
